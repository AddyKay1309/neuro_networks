FOUNDER STORY

One journey. A global mission.

At just sixteen years old, Neuro-Networks founder Aadhya Kumar transformed a deeply personal challenge into a movement dedicated to education, advocacy, and research. Born with a severe brachial plexus injury, she grew up navigating years of surgeries, rehabilitation, physical therapy, and countless questions about what she would or wouldn't be able to accomplish.

Rather than allowing those experiences to define her limits, they became the foundation for resilience. Every obstacle strengthened her belief that knowledge creates confidence, community creates hope, and research creates possibilities for future generations.

To share her story, Aadhya published Built Differently: Growing Up With a Brachial Plexus Injury, reflecting on childhood, recovery, perseverance, and the lessons learned from living with a peripheral nerve injury. The book encourages young patients and families to embrace resilience while reminding them they are never alone.

Through Neuro-Networks, her vision extends far beyond her own experience. She hopes that no child or family facing a peripheral nerve injury ever has to question their potential in isolation. By expanding awareness, supporting research, and connecting physicians, researchers, students, and families around the world, Neuro-Networks aims to accelerate discoveries that improve treatment and create brighter futures for every patient.

A comprehensive review published in 2026 highlights how peripheral nerve treatment is shifting beyond surgery alone. Researchers describe an integrated future combining neurotrophic growth factors, regenerative medicine, engineered nerve conduits, stem cell transplantation, electrical stimulation, pharmacological therapies, and targeted rehabilitation to improve nerve recovery after injury.

BIOMATERIALS

Magnesium May Transform Nerve Repair

Researchers are exploring biodegradable magnesium-based biomaterials that provide structural support while encouraging axonal regeneration, Schwann cell activity, angiogenesis, and reduced inflammation. Although clinical trials have not yet begun, these materials represent one of the most promising next-generation technologies in peripheral nerve repair.

New Approaches to Nerve Recovery

New treatments continue to explore advanced neuromodulation, regenerative medicine, platelet-rich plasma (PRP), stem cell therapies, improved rehabilitation protocols, and novel medications that target nerve growth and inflammation.
